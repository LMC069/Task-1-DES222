Smart Cities
Luke Colgrave
Smart cities represent the intersection of urban living and cutting-edge technology. With networks of sensors, public WiFi, and ubiquitous cellphone coverage, these cities promise enhanced livability through data-driven solutions. But as cities like Melbourne embrace this future, questions arise about the impact on privacy and community.

By making environmental data from public spaces accessible, citizens are empowered to interact with their surroundings in new ways. This project exemplifies how technology can enrich urban experiences while promoting transparency and community engagement.
Augmented Reality (AR) offers a glimpse into the future of smart cities. By overlaying digital content on physical spaces, AR can enhance the way we navigate and experience cities. However, the potential for a dystopian future, where reality is dominated by digital layers, cannot be ignored.
